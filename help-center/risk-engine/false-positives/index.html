handling false positives

what to do when a legitimate user's wallet is flagged as high risk.

why do false positives happen?

sometimes legitimate users unknowingly receive funds that originate from a flagged address. our engine identifies these direct on-chain links, which can result in a higher score than expected.

what to do

review the flags array in the scan response to understand which specific signals contributed to the score. consider whether the flagged behavior is consistent with legitimate use (e.g. high velocity from active defi usage).

if you believe a result is inaccurate, you can re-scan the address at a later date as on-chain activity changes over time. for questions about a specific result, contact us at support@sentinelpay.org with the wallet address and scan details.

common false positive scenarios

high_velocity on defi-active wallets

wallets that interact heavily with defi protocols — automated strategies, liquidity provision, or arbitrage bots — can legitimately generate more than 50 transactions in 24 hours. if your users include defi power users, treat this flag as a prompt for additional verification rather than an automatic block.

new_wallet on first-time users

legitimate new users registering for the first time will always have wallets under 30 days old. this flag adds only +20 points and is the weakest signal in the engine. never use it as a standalone block trigger — it should only shift a decision when combined with other flags.

io_imbalance on treasury or forwarding wallets

wallets used primarily to receive and forward funds — such as exchange hot wallets, salary distribution accounts, or treasury accounts — may show high io ratios without any malicious intent. at only +10 points, this flag has the lowest individual weight and rarely causes a score to cross a threshold on its own.

history_incomplete on high-activity wallets

if a wallet has more than 10,000 transactions of any type, the history_incomplete field in the response will be true. the score reflects only what was analyzed. for very active wallets, treat this as a signal for additional manual diligence rather than treating the score as definitive.

investigating a result

start with the flags array. each flag tells you which specific heuristic fired. cross-reference the wallet's on-chain activity with the trigger conditions documented in specific risk flags. re-scanning at a later date will reflect any new on-chain activity since the original scan.
